Nate Budzinski

About

I make films on the edges of genre — where horror meets world, the supernatural meets so-called reality.

The Hauntologist (2024), my scripted absurdist docufiction feature, has screened at festivals, cinemas, and art galleries. Gro's Garden, a folk horror feature film about an ambitious scientist who gets swallowed up by the Danish landscape, is in post-production for a late 2026 premiere. My documentary feature Nine Futures, about experimental and outsider music, premiered at CPH:DOX and screened internationally.

Alongside directing I've produced for stage and screen and online. My company, Wolf One, develops and produces films in collaboration with other filmmakers.

For fifteen years I wrote criticism on film, art and music for Sight & Sound, Frieze, The Wire and ArtReview — much of it on folk horror, hauntology and the occult revival, which is more or less where the films come from. Selected writing.

Born in BC. Aged in London. Based in Copenhagen.
